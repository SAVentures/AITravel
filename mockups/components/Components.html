01
Buttons
tier → style · one prominent per region

Pick by hierarchy tier, not by look. One prominent button per region — the blue primary is the accent's budgeted appearance. Secondary is a hairline outline; ghost is inline text; destructive is the only place a true red appears. Labels lead with a verb the user owns.

Glass circle buttons — bar chrome only

A single SF Symbol on the system glass material, 44×44 hit target. Tint conveys meaning — the one selected glyph carries blue; tinting all of them kills the signal.

02
Action bar
thumb-zone CTA · glass · floats over content

The screen's primary action, pinned in the bottom thumb zone — a glass bar holding the prominent blue CTA and an optional outline secondary. Content scrolls under it. The bar is chrome-thin; it hosts an action and recedes.

03
Place cards
definitive vs fuzzy · the product's one idea

The distinction the whole product turns on. A definitive place is solid and lifted: a white card with a rest shadow, a photo, a roman name, exact mono facts. A fuzzy place recedes: a flat grey surface, a glyph instead of a photo, an italic name, lighter ink. No borders anywhere — certainty is carried by elevation, weight, and italic.

04
Timeline rows
scan · trailing accessory = behavior contract

The workhorse for the day's stops. Leading mark → primary line + mono meta → a trailing accessory whose shape is a promise: chevron drills in, switch toggles in place, inline action does one thing, check selects. Between stops a transit connector rides the rail — a single mode (8 min walk), a multi-leg chain (train → walk), or a set of ways the AI weighs. Past stops fade — never strikethrough.
